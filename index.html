Let's see what is "Present Tense" & "Prsent Continuous Tense"

What is "Present Tense"?

"Simple Present Tense" is when you use a verb to tell about things that happen continually in the present.

Examples:

    He learns football on Sundays.

  • "learns" refers to "He," so need to add "s".
  • "on Sundays" means it is do regularly.

    • What is "Present Continuous Tense"?

    "Present Continuous Tense" is when you use a verb to tell about things that are happening right now.

    Examples:

      He is learning football now.

    • "is" learning refers to "He.
    • "now" means it is happening.

    Easy to distinguish "Present tense" & "Present continuous tense"...

    Present Tense
    Do regularly.

    Present Continuous Tense
    Happening.

    Keywords of Present Tense:

    Note: "Present Tense" is used with the following words:

    • always, every, usually
    • often, sometimes, seldom
    • never, on Sundays

    Keywords of Present Continuous Tense:

    Note: "Present Continuous Tense" is used with the following words:

    • now, currently, Presently
    • at the moment, at present, right now

    Prompt of "Present Tense" and "Present Continuous Tense"

    Present Tense

    • He/She/It: Verb + s/es/ies
    • I/You/We/They: Verb (no "s" needed)

    Present Continuous Tense

    • is/am/are + Verb(+ing)
